# Methods: Absolute Growth Rate Calculation

The Absolute Growth Rate (Linear Growth Rate) for each bag was calculated based on the change in dilution-dry mass corrected mean colony counts (plating data) over time.

## Formula
For a given bag, the absolute growth rate between two observations at times t_1 and t_2 (in days) is:

Rate = (N_t2 - N_t1) / (t_2 - t_1)

Where:
- N_t1 and N_t2 are the mean colony counts (cells per gram dry litter) at times t_1 and t_2 

## Data Processing
1. **Grouping:** Calculations were performed independently for each of the 12 unique bags (`bagid`).
2. **Temporal Alignment:** The calculated rate is assigned to the latter date t_2 of each interval.
3. **Scaling:** For visualization in the absolute growth plots, the values were scaled to 10^9 cells/day for improved readability.

## Data Source
Values derived from `Curtogrowth_data.csv` using the mean of columns `Colony_corrected_0` through `Colony_corrected_10-5`.
